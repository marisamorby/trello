So How Can I Help Trello?

Let me throw out some ideas that'll help you, and show you some of my past results.

Clear & Elegant Interfaces

Simplicity is important to me. That means the product needs to be beautiful but also work beautifully, too. To do this, I always design with the user in mind. How can I make their interaction with the site fun, easy, and memorable?

Collaboration & Teamwork

There's nothing better than working with others to brainstorm ideas and then start putting them into action. I've got a special knack for bringing teams together and keeping up motivation and focus.

Iterate & Make it Right

I'm all about design, iterate, design, iterate... until I've created the optimal design. That means I love getting feedback and talking to people about how I can make things work even better.

3 Marketing and Design Opportunities for Trello

Intuitive Interfaces

Designs so simple my mom could figure it out.

Let's look at the mobile interface and see how we can make it even easier to use Trello while you're out and about. We can make the comment section easier to exit, update the camera icon so it's more obvious, and add scroll bar visibility, just for a start.

Create the Trello habit

Let's make Trello the default habit for all lists.

We can gamify the onboarding process to make Trello the default choice when people are out shopping and want to remember a product for later, or when they're planning a project and need to collaborate and take notes.

Capitalize on Shareability

Make the product too good not to share.

Create resources, content, and designs that make sharing fun and easy. Engaging emails and social media updates about saving time, remote work, and team management will drive people back to the site and incite people to share.
